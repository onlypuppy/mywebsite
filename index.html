What is required (in no particular order)?

1) Bio

A Biography on the artist is required, including a section that explains where this concept album came from. What started it? Why? Etc. It is up to you to decide what is important, by way of important biographical information. For some artists, their home life as a child was / is instrumental for their musical career and persona, while for others, it can be glossed over.

Bio

2) Annotations

For at least two songs, you must complete annotations, explanations of the song as a singular entity and a piece of the greater concept album, is also required. More information on how to do these annotations, etc. will be provided a little later.

Annotations

3) Creative Connection

(Alliteration makes things sound soooo much cooler) – Artwork? Sure! Make a music video? I like it! Dance to a song? Impressive! Sing a cover? Maybe… (I will explain).

The whole point is to make a connection with the album or a song, provide insight and understanding in a creative way and upload it onto your site. An explanation of what you have ‘done’ may be needed… For example, a ‘cover’ of a song is not enough – why did you do what you did when you did what you did? Get it? You know, why hit those high notes at that time when Ol’ Blue Eyes didn’t? (Do you even know Ol’ Blue Eyes?).

Creative

4) Music links

Can be MP3 files, links to actual videos, you name it…. Figure it out.

Links

5) Analysis of Concept Album

You can call it an essay if you want, I see it more as an analysis. To me this will be the most challenging, ‘basic’ and exciting part. You are required to explain the Concept Album from front to back – take me on the journey throughout the album.

You should include lyrics / quotes from multiple songs as you explain the journey. For some, all songs will need to be explained individually, for others, songs may be ‘lumped together’. It is all dependent on you and your album.

Analysis

6) List of Works Cited

You are required to keep track of the sources that you use through your work. This will include the name of the author, the title of the article/webpage, and the URL from where it was found.

Cited
